## Why This Exists

Somewhere out there, a student is trying to learn Linux. They search, they dig, they find — but almost everything worth reading is in English. The good tutorials, the deep dives, the practical guides — all English. If you are lucky enough to read it fluently, great. But many aren't, and that gap has a cost.

We built this wiki because that gap bothered us.

---

## What We Believe

**Knowledge should have no language barrier.**
Linux, open source, DevOps, system internals — this knowledge belongs to everyone. Not just to those who happen to read English comfortably. Every developer, student, and engineer in the Arab world deserves access to the same quality of resources as anyone else.

**One place is better than a hundred tabs.**
The Arabic internet is full of scattered bits — a YouTube video here, a forum post there, a half-finished blog from 2015. We want to fix that. One place, well organized, always growing, where you can go from zero to deep without losing the thread.

**Community beats any single author.**
No one person knows everything. But a community of curious people — students, professionals, beginners, veterans — knows quite a lot. This wiki is built on that idea. Every contribution, no matter how small, makes it better for everyone who comes after.

**Contribution is a skill worth learning.**
This wiki is written in English — on purpose. Not because Arabic doesn't matter, but because contributing in English teaches you something. You learn Git, pull requests, code review, open source workflows. The same skills that get you hired, that let you contribute to any project in the world. We want this wiki to be your first open source contribution, not your last.

---

## What We Cover

This is not just a "how to install Linux" guide. We go further:

- Linux fundamentals and the command line
- The boot process and system internals
- File systems, permissions, processes, and networking
- Shell scripting and text processing
- Package managers and system administration
- Git and GitHub workflows
- DevOps tools — Docker, orchestration, and beyond
- Embedded Linux and low-level system topics

If it runs on Linux and someone needs to understand it, it belongs here.

---

## Who This Is For

This wiki is for the student who just installed their first Linux distro and has no idea what they're looking at.

It is for the developer who wants to stop being afraid of the terminal.

It is for the DevOps engineer who needs a reliable reference they can trust.

It is for the curious mind who wants to understand not just *how* things work, but *why*.

And it is especially for the Arab who looked at the open source world and thought — *I want to be part of this, but I don't know where to start.* This is where you start.

---

## An Invitation

This wiki exists because people chose to contribute. Every page was written by someone who decided their knowledge was worth sharing. If you have learned something — from this wiki, from experience, from anywhere — you can pass it on.

Open a pull request. Fix a typo. Write a new page. Translate a concept you finally understood. You don't have to be an expert. You just have to care.

The Arab open source community is growing. Be part of building it.
